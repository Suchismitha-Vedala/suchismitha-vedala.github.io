Thesis

Enterprise AI needs an operating model, not a collection of demos.

Agents need structure

Useful agents need tools, context, permissions, handoffs, observability, error handling, and workflow boundaries. I focus on reusable agent patterns that can survive real enterprise processes.

LLMs need routing

One model is rarely the right answer for every task. Prompt classification, model scoring, adaptive routing, and feedback loops make AI systems more resilient, cost-aware, and fit for purpose.

Scale needs economics

Tokens, latency, quality, and model choice are operating variables. Token optimization, compression, caching, rate governance, and cost visibility turn usage into managed infrastructure.

Adoption needs trust

Evaluation, observability, lifecycle controls, governance, and ROI measurement make enterprise AI credible to builders, leaders, risk teams, and business owners.
